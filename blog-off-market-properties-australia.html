Off-Market Properties: How Australian Buyers Access Hidden Deals Before They're Listed

While most buyers compete for the same properties on Domain and realestate.com.au, savvy buyers and their advocates are transacting on a completely different playing field — one the public never sees.

RM

Open your property app on a Saturday morning. Scroll through the listings. Attend the open homes. Submit your offer. Lose at auction. Repeat. For most Australian buyers, this is the cycle — exhausting, competitive, emotionally draining, and often futile in tight markets. But there's a parallel property market operating quietly beneath the surface, and buyers with the right connections never have to enter the public arena at all.

Off-market property transactions — where a property changes hands without ever being publicly listed — account for an estimated 15 to 25 percent of all Australian residential transactions in any given year. In premium suburbs, prestige markets, and during periods of tight supply, that figure climbs even higher. Understanding how off-market works, and how to access it, could fundamentally change your buying experience.

What Does Off-Market Actually Mean?

An off-market property is one that is available for sale but has not been publicly advertised on the major listing portals — Domain, realestate.com.au, or similar platforms. The seller may be working with a selling agent who quietly approaches selected potential buyers, or they may be working directly with a buyers agent who has expressed prior interest in properties of that type in that location.

Off-market is not the same as "pocket listing" (a controversial practice where agents don't properly market a property to maximise the vendor's outcome) — true off-market transactions occur with the seller's informed consent, and the seller typically has specific reasons for not wanting a public campaign.

There are also "pre-market" properties — homes where a selling agent is conducting quiet due diligence and early-buyer testing before a formal campaign launch. If a buyer is found at an acceptable price pre-campaign, the vendor saves on marketing costs and achieves their goal without the uncertainty of a public auction. Buyers agents with strong agent relationships are typically the first calls made when a property enters this pre-market phase.

Why Do Sellers Choose Off-Market?

Selling off-market is not always about achieving the highest price — it's often about control, privacy, and convenience. Understanding the seller's motivation helps buyers and their advocates approach these transactions with the right strategy.

Privacy: High-profile individuals, executives, and people going through significant life events (divorce, estate administration, financial difficulty) often don't want their home appearing on public portals with a marketing campaign. The thought of thousands of strangers walking through their home on a Saturday morning is unappealing at the best of times.

Cost savings: A full marketing campaign — professional photography, video, floor plans, online listings, print advertising, auctioneer fees — can cost a vendor $15,000 to $40,000 or more on a premium property. Selling off-market eliminates most of these costs, which means the vendor may be prepared to accept a slightly lower price and still net the same amount.

Speed and certainty: Some vendors need to transact quickly — they've already bought elsewhere and need to release equity, or they need certainty of outcome rather than the risk of a passed-in auction. An off-market transaction with a ready buyer can be exchanged within days.

Testing the market: Vendors sometimes test buyer appetite off-market before committing to a public campaign. If they find a buyer at an acceptable price, they transact. If not, they proceed to market — often with a clearer sense of realistic pricing.

Why Buyers Agents Have Access — and You Don't

Selling agents don't call random members of the public when an off-market opportunity arises. They call buyers agents who have already demonstrated that they have qualified, ready buyers. This is the fundamental reason why buyers agents provide off-market access that individual buyers cannot replicate on their own.

A well-networked buyers agent has relationships with dozens — sometimes hundreds — of selling agents across their target markets. These relationships are built on trust and track record: the selling agent knows that when a buyers agent says their client is ready to transact, they mean it. Referrals move quickly, and if a buyers agent wastes a selling agent's time with an unqualified buyer, they won't receive the next call.

On-Market vs Off-Market: An Honest Comparison

Off-Market Advantages

  • Little or no competition from other buyers
  • More time to conduct due diligence without pressure
  • Negotiating directly with vendor — no auction heat
  • Seller often motivated by speed, not just price
  • Properties may be underpriced (vendor saving on campaign costs)
  • Access to properties that never appear publicly

Off-Market Considerations

  • Fewer comparable sales — harder to assess fair value
  • Vendor may not have tested true market price
  • No auction transparency — price discovery is harder
  • Requires trust in your buyers agent's valuation
  • Fewer properties available at any one time
  • May require faster decision-making

How to Find Off-Market Properties

Beyond engaging a buyers agent, there are several strategies individual buyers can attempt — though each has significant limitations without professional relationships.

Buyers agents: By far the most effective method. A good buyers agent's primary value proposition is off-market access. If you're serious about buying in a competitive market, this is where your investment in professional advocacy pays back fastest.

Direct mail campaigns: Some buyers send letters to property owners in their target streets or suburbs, expressing interest in purchasing. This can occasionally work in established owner-occupier suburbs where residents have lived for decades and aren't actively thinking about selling. The response rate is typically below 1%, so it requires significant volume and patience.

Building relationships with local agents: Register your interest with local selling agents and ask to be notified of off-market opportunities. Be specific about your brief, budget, and readiness. Agents will prioritise buyers they believe are serious and qualified — having a written pre-approval and demonstrating genuine buying intent is essential.

Deceased estate networks: Solicitors and estate administrators often work quietly with trusted agents and buyers agents when managing the sale of a deceased estate. These properties frequently transact off-market to minimise distress and administration complexity.

Is the Price Right? Managing Off-Market Risk

The primary risk in an off-market transaction is overpaying — if there's no competition or public price discovery, how do you know the agreed price is fair? This is where an experienced buyers agent's valuation skills are critical. A good buyers agent will conduct a thorough comparable sales analysis before recommending a purchase price, drawing on recent sales data, site-specific factors, and market knowledge. They will also advise you on what the property would likely have sold for had it gone to public campaign — and whether the off-market price represents genuine value or simply convenience.

Never purchase an off-market property without conducting the full due diligence you would on any other property — building and pest inspection, contract review, title search, and independent valuation if the deal is significant enough to warrant it. Off-market does not mean risk-free.

The Bottom Line

Off-market property access is one of the most tangible advantages that buyers agents provide. In competitive markets — and Australian capital city markets have been relentlessly competitive through much of the 2020s — the ability to access a meaningful portion of available properties before they reach the public listing portals is a structural advantage that cannot easily be replicated by individual buyers.

Whether you're a first home buyer trying to avoid the auction circus or an investor building a portfolio strategically, understanding and accessing the off-market segment of the market could be the factor that determines whether you buy — and at what price.
