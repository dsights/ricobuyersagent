What Is a Buyer's Agent?

A buyer's agent (also known as a buyer's advocate) is a licensed professional who acts exclusively on behalf of the property buyer. Unlike a real estate agent — who works for the seller and is legally obligated to get the highest possible price — a buyer's agent is 100% on your side. Their sole job is to help you find the right property at the right price, on the best possible terms.

At RICO, we never represent a seller. We have zero conflicts of interest. Our only obligation is to you.

How a Buyer's Agent Saves You Time

The average self-directed property buyer in Australia spends 6–12 months searching for a property. RICO clients typically find and secure theirs in 4–8 weeks. Here's how:

  • Off-market and pre-market access: We have relationships with 500+ selling agents who alert us to properties before they're listed publicly. You see opportunities others never know about.
  • Targeted search, not random browsing: We don't waste your time on unsuitable properties. Every property we present meets your exact brief — location, price, property type, and investment criteria.
  • We attend inspections on your behalf: No more spending every Saturday at open homes. We inspect, assess, and report back — with detailed video walkthroughs for remote buyers.
  • We manage all paperwork: Contracts, building reports, strata documents — we coordinate everything so you don't have to.

How a Buyer's Agent Saves You Money

It might seem counterintuitive to pay a professional to help you buy — but the numbers consistently show that buyer's agents save their clients far more than their fee.

  • Expert negotiation: We know every tactic selling agents use. We approach price negotiation with data — comparable sales, days on market, vendor motivation — not emotion. Our clients consistently pay below the initial asking price.
  • Avoiding costly mistakes: We identify structural issues, flood zones, bad strata, unfavourable contracts, and overpriced properties before you make an offer. One avoided mistake pays for our fee many times over.
  • Access to off-market deals: Properties that aren't on the open market often transact at better prices — because there's no competitive bidding process pushing prices up artificially.
  • Auction strategy: Without a professional bidder, emotional buyers routinely overpay at auction. RICO advocates bid calmly, strategically, and within your pre-set limit — removing the pressure that costs buyers thousands.

The RICO guarantee: We work only for buyers, never sellers. No referral fees, no kickbacks from developers, no conflicts of interest. Our fee is fixed and disclosed upfront — and our clients consistently tell us they saved more than our fee through better negotiation alone.

What Does the RICO Process Look Like?

  1. Free Strategy Session: A 30-minute consultation to understand your goals, budget, preferred locations, and timeline.
  2. Property Brief: We document exactly what you need and begin our search — advertised, pre-market, and off-market.
  3. Shortlisting and Inspection: We present only properties that match your brief, with detailed reports and video walkthroughs.
  4. Due Diligence: Building reports, strata reports, comparable sales — we verify every detail before you commit.
  5. Negotiation or Auction: We negotiate price and terms on your behalf, or bid at auction with a clear strategy.
  6. Settlement: We coordinate with your solicitor and lender right through to keys in hand.
