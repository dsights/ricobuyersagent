7 Signs You're Ready to Buy Your First Home

Buying your first home is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll ever make. So, how do you know if you're ready to take the plunge?

It's not just about having a deposit saved. There are several other financial and personal factors to consider. Here are seven signs that you're ready to buy your first home.

1. You Have a Stable Income

Lenders want to see a history of stable employment and a reliable income. If you've been in your job for a while and have a steady flow of income, you're in a good position to apply for a home loan.

2. You Have a Healthy Deposit

While it's possible to get a loan with a 5% deposit, a larger deposit will put you in a much stronger position. A 20% deposit will help you avoid Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) and could give you access to better interest rates.

3. You're on Top of Your Debts

If you have a lot of personal debt, such as credit card debt or car loans, it can be difficult to get a home loan. Lenders want to see that you can manage your finances responsibly.

4. You Have an Emergency Fund

Homeownership comes with unexpected costs, such as repairs and maintenance. Having an emergency fund will give you peace of mind and help you deal with any surprises that come your way.

5. You're Ready to Settle Down

Buying a home is a long-term commitment. If you're planning on moving to a new city or changing careers in the near future, it might not be the right time to buy.

6. You've Done Your Research

You understand the local property market, you know what you can afford, and you've researched the home buying process. You're not going in blind.

7. You're Emotionally Ready

Buying a home can be an emotional rollercoaster. You need to be prepared for the highs and lows of the process, from the excitement of finding your dream home to the disappointment of missing out at auction.

Ready to Take the Next Step?

If you've ticked all these boxes, you might be ready to buy your first home. But you don't have to do it alone. A buyer's agent can help you assess your readiness, make a plan, and find the right property for you.
