Why Australia Remains a Premier International Investment Market

Despite the 2026 budget changes and elevated interest rates, Australia's fundamentals for international investors are as strong as they have ever been:

  • Political stability: Australia is a stable AAA-rated democracy with strong rule of law and transparent property rights. Your investment is protected.
  • Transparent market: Australian property transactions are registered, regulated, and publicly recorded. No opacity or counterparty risk.
  • English language: Legal documents, contracts, and market information are in English — accessible to buyers from most major expat markets.
  • Population growth: 423,600 new residents in a single year. That demand is structural and ongoing.
  • Housing shortage: 262,000 fewer homes than needed by 2029. Scarcity protects your investment.
  • Strong rental yields: With vacancy at 1.0%, rental demand is intense. Finding quality tenants is not a challenge.
  • AUD diversification: For USD, GBP, or SGD investors, Australian dollar-denominated property provides geographic and currency diversification.

What the 2026 Budget Changed for International Buyers

The 2026 Budget introduced two key changes with direct relevance for international buyers:

What Changed (Less Favourable)
  • Foreign buyer ban extended: Non-residents cannot buy established dwellings until 30 June 2029. Existing owners not affected.
  • New build remains available: But established properties are off-limits for foreign non-residents — a continuation and extension of the pre-existing ban.
What Didn't Change (Still Favourable)
  • New builds fully available: FIRB-approved foreign buyers can still purchase new dwellings and off-the-plan apartments.
  • Full negative gearing on new builds: Investors in new construction retain the old negative gearing rules.
  • Expat Australians fully exempt: Australian citizens living abroad can buy any property with zero FIRB restrictions.

FIRB: What Every Foreign Buyer Needs to Know in 2026

The Foreign Investment Review Board (FIRB) approves foreign investment in Australian residential property. If you are a non-resident, you must obtain FIRB approval before signing any contract.

The FIRB Application Process

Standard FIRB applications process in approximately 30 days. Applications must be submitted before signing a contract. Application fees start at $14,100 for properties up to $1 million. RICO works with registered FIRB specialists who can advise on your eligibility, prepare applications, and manage the approval timeline to align with your property purchase.

Currency Considerations: AUD in 2026

For non-AUD investors, the Australian dollar's current level is a factor worth understanding. The AUD typically trades between 0.60–0.75 USD over recent cycles. At around 0.645 in mid-2026, AUD is at a historically reasonable entry point for USD investors.

Key currency considerations:

  • Forward contracts: Lock in today's exchange rate for settlement in 3–6 months through specialist forex brokers.
  • AUD timing: Many analysts expect AUD to strengthen as the RBA eventually cuts rates and commodity prices remain elevated — benefiting USD/GBP holders who bought at current rates.
  • Rental income: If you're an overseas investor, Australian rental income is paid in AUD — providing ongoing currency diversification even after purchase.
