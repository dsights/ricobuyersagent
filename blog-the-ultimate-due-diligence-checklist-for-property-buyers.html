The Ultimate Due Diligence Checklist for Property Buyers

A comprehensive 20-point checklist for property due diligence — from building inspections to strata reports and comparable sales analysis.

PW
Piyush Wagji — Founder
RICO Buyers Agent · 3+ Years Experience

Buying a property is a huge investment. That's why it's so important to do your homework before you sign on the dotted line. Due diligence is the process of investigating a property to make sure there are no hidden surprises. It's your opportunity to uncover any potential issues that could cost you thousands down the track.

To help you on your way, we've created the ultimate due diligence checklist for property buyers.

Financial Preparation

  • Get Pre-Approval: Know your borrowing capacity before you start looking.
  • Budget for All Costs: Don't forget to factor in stamp duty, legal fees, and other associated costs.
  • Have an Emergency Fund: Be prepared for unexpected expenses.

Location and Market Research

  • Get to Know the Suburb: Spend time in the area to get a feel for the lifestyle and community.
  • Research Recent Sales: Understand the local market and what properties are worth.
  • Check for Local Developments: Find out about any planned developments that could impact the area.

Legal and Conveyancing

  • Review the Contract of Sale: Have your solicitor or conveyancer review the contract before you sign anything.
  • Check the Title: Make sure the property has a clear title with no outstanding caveats or easements.
  • Investigate Zoning and Planning: Understand any restrictions on how you can use the property.

Property and Building Inspections

  • Arrange a Building and Pest Inspection: This is a non-negotiable. A professional inspection can uncover a wide range of potential issues.
  • Check for Structural Issues: Look for cracks in the walls, uneven floors, and other signs of structural problems.
  • Inspect the Plumbing and Electricals: Make sure everything is in good working order.
  • Look for Signs of Water Damage: Water damage can be a sign of a bigger problem, such as a leaking roof or plumbing issues.

Your Due Diligence Partner

Due diligence can be a time-consuming and complex process. But it's a crucial step in the property buying journey. A buyer's agent can be your trusted partner in this process, managing all aspects of due diligence on your behalf.
