How the First Home Guarantee Actually Works

The First Home Guarantee is a government guarantee, not a cash grant. The government does not give you money — instead, it guarantees the gap between your 5% deposit and the 20% that lenders normally require before waiving LMI.

Without the scheme, a buyer with 5% deposit would pay Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) — a one-time premium that insures the lender against default. On an $800,000 purchase, LMI can cost $25,000–$42,000. The scheme eliminates this cost entirely.

Who Is Eligible in 2026?

After the 2026 Budget expansion, the eligibility criteria are simpler and more inclusive than ever:

  • Australian citizen (including dual citizens) or permanent resident
  • First home buyer — you (and your co-buyer, if any) must never have previously owned residential property in Australia
  • Owner-occupier intent — you must live in the property (not rent it out initially)
  • 5% genuine savings — lender will verify this through statements
  • Property within the price cap for your state/territory and location
  • No income cap — the previous $125K single / $200K couple limit has been removed (Budget 2026)

The income cap removal is a major win. Previously, a couple earning $210,000 combined was excluded. After the 2026 Budget, they are fully eligible. Many dual-income households in capital cities who previously assumed they were ineligible should revisit their eligibility today.

The Help to Buy Scheme: Taking It Further

For buyers who qualify (income under $100K single / $160K couple), the Help to Buy shared equity scheme is even more powerful than the 5% Deposit Scheme. The government contributes:

  • Up to 40% of the purchase price for new builds
  • Up to 30% of the purchase price for established homes
  • Minimum 2% deposit required from the buyer

This means you can buy a $750,000 home with just $15,000 — a 2% deposit. The government owns a 30–40% share, and you gradually buy it back as your income allows. There is no rent on the government's share — you simply pay to buy them out over time.

  • Best for: Lower-income buyers with minimal savings

Step-by-Step: How to Access the 5% Deposit Scheme

  1. Confirm eligibility: First home buyer status, Australian citizenship/PR, intent to live in the property, property within price cap.
  2. Build your 5% deposit: At a minimum. Lenders will check 3–6 months of savings history (genuine savings). First home buyer savings accounts, gifts from family, and FHSS (First Home Super Saver) withdrawals may qualify — ask your mortgage broker.
  3. Apply through a participating lender: Only First Home Guarantee-accredited lenders can process the scheme. The NHFIC website has the current list. Major banks and most lenders participate.
  4. Get pre-approval: The pre-approval confirms your maximum purchase price and guarantee eligibility. This is your green light to begin serious property searching.
  5. Engage a buyer's agent: RICO advocates search on-market and off-market properties within your pre-approved budget and state price cap. We negotiate the price and terms, and coordinate the contract.
  6. Settle: The guarantee activates at settlement. No LMI invoice — it simply does not apply.
