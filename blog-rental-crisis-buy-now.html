The Rental Crisis Case for Buying Now: Why Renting Is Costing You More Than a Mortgage

At $697 per week nationally and rising 7.3% annually, rent isn't just expensive today — it's a wealth-destroying spiral. Here's the maths that proves buying now makes financial sense, even at 6.5% interest rates.

SA
Sarah A. — Senior Buyer Advocate

Australia's rental market in 2026 is, by virtually every measure, the worst it has ever been for tenants. Vacancy rates sit at 1.0% nationally — in acute shortage territory. Rents rose 7.3% in the past year, more than twice the pace of wage growth. In capital cities, the median rent has crossed $794 per week.

And every year you rent instead of own, the gap between where you are and where you want to be grows wider. Let us show you exactly how wide — with real numbers.

The Rent Trap: Why It Gets Harder Every Year You Wait

Here is the cruel arithmetic of the rental market in 2026. As rent rises at 7.3% annually and wages grow at 3.4%, each passing year has two effects:

  1. Your rent bill consumes more of your income — leaving less to save for a deposit
  2. Property values rise — meaning the deposit you need grows faster than you can save it

For a renter saving $1,000 per month toward a 10% deposit on a $850,000 property ($85,000 target), the property appreciates approximately $59,500 in the same year their deposit grows by $12,000. The gap widens by nearly $47,000 every year they delay.

The Numbers Behind the Rent Trap

At the national median rent of $697/week ($36,244/year), a renter is spending $36K annually on housing with zero return. A buyer making the same payment is directing a portion of every dollar toward equity — and capturing capital growth on top. After 10 years, the financial divergence between renter and buyer becomes almost impossible to close through savings alone.

What 5 More Years of Renting Actually Costs

If you're currently renting at the national median and delay buying for 5 years, here's what that decision costs in real terms:

The Cost of Waiting 5 More Years
  • Additional rent paid: ~$221,000 over 5 years at 7.3% growth
  • Property value increase: ~$335,000 on $850K at 7% annual growth
  • Larger deposit needed: $33,500 more for the same 10% deposit
  • Total wealth gap created: ~$556,000 worse off than if you had bought today
The Benefit of Buying Today
  • Equity in 5 years: ~$220,000+ (deposit + principal repaid + growth)
  • Mortgage stability: Housing cost is fixed, not subject to landlord rent hikes
  • Security: Can't be asked to vacate. No lease renewals. Your home.
  • Rate scenario: If rates fall 1%, repayments drop ~$380/month on $700K loan

The Fastest Path to Ownership in 2026

For renters ready to act, here is the most efficient route to ownership given the current environment:

  • Step 1 — Use the 5% Deposit Scheme: Instead of 20%, you only need 5%. For an $800K home, that's $40,000 vs $160,000. Get in the market faster.
  • Step 2 — Consider rentvesting: If you can't yet buy where you want to live, buy where the numbers work. Rent in your preferred area while your investment property builds equity.
  • Step 3 — Engage a buyer's agent: Professional representation saves you time, emotion, and typically 3–8% on purchase price. On an $800K property, that's $24K–$64K.
  • Step 4 — Target investor-retreating suburbs: The budget has softened demand in apartment-heavy, investor-favoured suburbs. That's where the short-term buying opportunity is sharpest.

Rent assistance doesn't solve the problem: Commonwealth Rent Assistance has been increased in recent budgets, but at a maximum of ~$185/fortnight, it barely covers one week's rent increase from 2025 to 2026. Government assistance helps the most vulnerable — but it doesn't build your wealth.
