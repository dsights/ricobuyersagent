How to Win a Real Estate Auction

Expert strategies from RICO buyer advocates — how to bid with confidence, control emotion, and win at auction without overpaying.

RM
Rico M. — Principal Buyer Advocate
RICO Buyers Agent · 15+ Years Experience

The auctioneer's hammer falls, the crowd erupts, and a new homeowner is born. For many, this is the thrilling conclusion to their property search. But for the unprepared, a real estate auction can be a stressful and costly experience.

With the right preparation and strategy, you can enter the auction room with confidence and give yourself the best possible chance of success. Here's our buyer's guide to winning a real estate auction in Australia.

1. Pre-Auction Preparation: The Key to Success

Winning an auction starts long before auction day. Thorough preparation is essential.

  • Get Your Finances in Order: Auctions are unconditional, so you need to have your finances sorted. Get pre-approval for a loan so you know exactly how much you can borrow.
  • Do Your Due Diligence: Arrange building and pest inspections and have your solicitor or conveyancer review the contract of sale. You need to be aware of any potential issues before you bid.
  • Know the Property's Value: Research recent sales in the area to get a clear idea of the property's market value. This will help you set a realistic budget and avoid overpaying.
  • Attend Other Auctions: Get a feel for the auction process by attending a few auctions in the area. Observe the auctioneers, the bidders, and the overall atmosphere.

2. Auction Day Strategy: Play to Win

On auction day, it's all about staying calm, sticking to your plan, and bidding with confidence.

  • Set Your "Walk-Away" Price: Decide on your absolute maximum price and stick to it. Don't get caught up in the excitement and bid more than you can afford.
  • Project Confidence: Bid loudly and clearly. Make eye contact with the auctioneer. Even if you're nervous, try to project an air of confidence.
  • Vary Your Bidding Increments: Don't be afraid to make smaller bids as you get closer to your limit. This can signal to other bidders that you're nearing your maximum.
  • Read the Room: Pay attention to the other bidders. Look for signs that they're nearing their limit, such as hesitation or conferring with their partner.

3. Understanding the Rules of the Game

It's important to understand the key rules of an Australian real estate auction.

  • Unconditional Sale: When the hammer falls, the property is sold. There is no cooling-off period.
  • Vendor Bids: The auctioneer can make bids on behalf of the seller (vendor bids). These must be announced.
  • Reserve Price: This is the minimum price that the seller is willing to accept. The property will not be sold until the bidding reaches the reserve price.

4. What if the Property Passes In?

If the bidding doesn't reach the reserve price, the property is "passed in". As the highest bidder, you'll usually have the first right to negotiate with the seller. This can be a great opportunity to secure the property at a good price.
