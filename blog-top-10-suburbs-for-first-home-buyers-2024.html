Top 10 Suburbs for First Home Buyers in Australia in 2024

The Australian property market can be a daunting landscape for first-home buyers. Rising interest rates and cost-of-living pressures have made affordability a major hurdle. However, with a surge in new listings and the right strategy, 2024 could be the year you finally step onto the property ladder.

To help you on your journey, we've identified 10 of the most promising suburbs for first-home buyers across Australia. These suburbs offer a combination of affordability, lifestyle, and growth potential.

Top 10 Suburbs for First Home Buyers

  1. Tarneit, Victoria: Located in Melbourne's outer west, Tarneit is a popular choice for families, with a median house price of around $650,000. It offers a range of new housing developments and good transport links to the city.
  2. Parramatta, New South Wales: If you're looking for an affordable entry into the Sydney market, Parramatta is a great option. While houses are expensive, units are more accessible, and the suburb boasts a bustling CBD, excellent restaurants, and major transport connections.
  3. Greenbank, Queensland: Situated in the high-growth corridor between Brisbane and the Gold Coast, Greenbank is in high demand. It offers a mix of new housing estates and larger, established properties, with good access to both cities.
  4. Girrawheen, Western Australia: Perth has been a top-performing market in 2024, and Girrawheen is a standout for affordability and growth. This northern suburb has seen strong price growth in both houses and units.
  5. Elizabeth Downs, South Australia: For those on a tight budget, Elizabeth Downs in Adelaide's north offers incredible value. It has experienced significant house price growth in recent times, making it a smart investment.
  6. Glenorchy, Tasmania: Just a short drive from Hobart, Glenorchy is a popular choice for first-home buyers in Tasmania. It offers a range of affordable houses and units, with good local amenities.
  7. Woodroffe, Northern Territory: If you're looking to buy in the Top End, Woodroffe is one of the most affordable suburbs in Darwin. It's a family-friendly area with good schools and parks.
  8. Belconnen, Australian Capital Territory: As a major town centre in Canberra, Belconnen offers a wide range of housing options, from affordable units to family homes. It has excellent amenities, including a major shopping centre and a university.
  9. Ferntree Gully, Victoria: Nestled in the foothills of the Dandenong Ranges, Ferntree Gully offers a more affordable entry point into Melbourne's desirable outer east. It's a leafy suburb with a strong sense of community.
  10. Marsden Park, New South Wales: Located in Sydney's growing north-west, Marsden Park is a hub of new housing development. It's a popular choice for young families, with new schools, parks, and shopping centres.

Government Support for First Home Buyers

The Australian government offers several schemes to help first-home buyers enter the market. These include:

  • First Home Super Saver Scheme: Allows you to save money for your first home inside your super fund.
  • Home Guarantee Scheme: Enables you to buy a home with a deposit as low as 5% without paying Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI).
  • First Home Owner Grant (FHOG): A one-off grant for eligible first-home buyers. The amount and eligibility criteria vary by state and territory.
